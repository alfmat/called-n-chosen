The Eucharist and the Paschal Mystery of Christ

- Matt Sebastian

When teaching a youth group in our Parish, I usually define Paschal mystery as the work of the Triune God, in the Crucifixion, Resurrection and Glorious Ascension of Christ. God, The Father is with Jesus throughout his earthly life, even unto his death, and subsequently in His resurrection and ascension and exaltation to be seated at the right hand of God (cf. prophesied in Psalms 110.1 and Acts 26:23, also cf. Marthaler p197, 199). Further, it was essential that the Son depart the earth and be raised up this way, for God the Father and the Son to send down the Holy Spirit to sanctify the earth and bring about salvation of mankind (cf. John 16:7).

Every time we partake in the Eucharistic celebration, we re-live the paschal mystery of the sacrificial death, resurrection and ascension of our Lord, Jesus. To further explain, the Eucharist for us Catholics, is not merely symbolic, but by the power of the Holy Spirit transubstantiated into the ‘real’ body and blood of Christ that we consume. The more we understand this, we would find every Mass to be a renewed way to partake in this paschal mystery and experience this “real” presence of our Lord in the bread and wine we consume. It is beyond our human fathoming to realize the extent of God’s love for us, even to the point that he wants to be within us in physical communion, through our consumption of the Eucharistic body of blood of Christ. When we realize this magnanimity of God’s love and how His Body and Blood can wash away our iniquities and cleanse us with His saving grace, then it becomes easy to partake in the Eucharistic celebration joyfully and whole-heartedly, rather than treat it as a Sunday ritual.
