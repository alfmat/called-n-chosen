Why be Baptized?

- Matt Sebastian

To add to some of the discussion points, Baptism initiates us into the Christian Church. Marthaler (p. 329 ’87 edition) speaks to the association of Baptism to the ‘Forgiveness of Sins’ especially in the Eastern creeds and further substantiates this by references to Peter’s answer to disciples on Pentecost affirming repentance and being baptized for the remission of sins (cf. Acts 2:38). Further, the Apostolic Tradition of St. Hippolytus emphasizes the correlation between baptism and the remission of sins. Although this remission from sins during Baptism is a one-time cleansing during initiation into Christianity, we need our Lord’s daily saving grace to see us through our earthly life and give us continual protection from the effects of sin committed hence. This is where ongoing repentance and the sacrament of reconciliation play a huge part in developing in us a transformative change in heart (signified by the Greek word ‘metanoia’). The resultant cleansing and purity in heart enables us to partake in the Eucharist in a worthy manner. This renews the grace of the Holy Spirit in us on a continual basis, strengthens our faith and draws us closer in our relationship with Christ.
